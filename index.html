About the project

We do more than collect waste — we change daily habits.

Plastic pollution is not only a cleanliness issue; it affects public spaces, drains, animals, soil, and the pride we feel in our neighbourhoods. Our project brings students and residents together so Rajkot’s localities become cleaner from the inside out.

How our weekend visits work

1

Select a locality

Each weekend, we choose a different area of Rajkot and identify streets, open spaces, and common litter spots where plastic waste collects.

2

Collect and sort plastic

We safely collect bottles, wrappers, carry bags, caps, packaging, and containers, then separate them by type and condition.

3

Talk to residents

We explain why littering harms the locality and share simple alternatives such as cloth bags, refill bottles, and better segregation.

4

Reuse creatively

Clean plastic is recycled at home into planters, organizers, eco-bricks, decorations, and other useful objects.

Our message to Rajkot

A clean city is not created by one group in one day. It is created when every person refuses unnecessary plastic, disposes of waste responsibly, and inspires one more person to do the same.

Our project is persuasive because it is practical: instead of only saying “do not litter,” we show people what discarded plastic can become. A bottle can become a planter. A wrapper can become part of an eco-brick. A household can become a zero-litter example for the street.

Step-by-step guide

How to recycle collected plastic waste at home

Follow this safe and simple process to turn suitable plastic waste into useful home items. Always work with clean plastic and adult supervision when cutting or using heat.

Sort the plastic

Separate bottles, caps, containers, wrappers, and bags. Keep recyclable-looking plastic apart from dirty, sharp, medical, or hazardous waste. Avoid unknown chemical containers.

Wash and dry completely

Rinse with soap and water to remove food, smell, and dirt. Dry in sunlight so the plastic does not grow mold and is easier to reuse.

Choose a useful project

Pick an item based on the plastic type: bottles can become planters, caps can become art, containers can become organizers, and soft plastic can be packed into eco-bricks.

Cut, shape, and smooth edges

Use scissors or a cutter carefully. Smooth sharp edges with tape, sandpaper, or folded cloth. Do not burn plastic, as fumes can be harmful.

Create the recycled item

Decorate with paint, fabric, jute string, labels, or natural materials. Try bottle planters, pen stands, storage boxes, bird feeders, seedling pots, or wall decorations.

Use, gift, or display it

Place recycled items at home, school, or community spaces. Add a small label explaining what waste was used so others feel inspired to recycle too.

Safety note: Do not melt or burn plastic at home. Avoid recycling medical waste, pesticide containers, chemical bottles, or heavily contaminated plastic. If an item cannot be safely reused, send it to an authorized waste collector or recycling channel.
Individual strategies

How every person in Rajkot can reduce plastic waste

Small personal choices become powerful when an entire locality practices them together. Start with these realistic habits.

🛍️

Carry a cloth bag

Keep one bag in your school bag, scooter, car, or near the door so you never need a plastic carry bag while shopping.

💧

Use a refill bottle

Carry a reusable water bottle and avoid buying single-use plastic bottles during tuition, travel, sports, or shopping.

🥡

Say no to extra packaging

Choose loose items where possible and politely refuse unnecessary straws, spoons, cups, and plastic wrappers.

🗑️

Segregate at home

Use separate bins for dry waste and wet waste. Clean dry plastic has a much better chance of being recycled.

🏪

Support responsible shops

Encourage local vendors to use paper, cloth, reusable containers, or returnable packaging instead of thin plastic bags.

👥

Influence one more person

Share the message with family, neighbours, classmates, and shopkeepers. A locality changes faster when people remind each other kindly.
